## German Capitalization Rules Applied

### 1. Nouns - Always Capitalized
- **Rule**: All German nouns must be capitalized regardless of position in sentence
- **Examples**: der Hund, das Haus, die Arbeit, der Tisch

### 2. Verbs - Always Lowercase
- **Rule**: German verb infinitives are lowercase
- **Examples**: arbeiten, gehen, machen, sprechen

### 3. Adjectives - Always Lowercase
- **Rule**: German adjectives are lowercase unless used as substantiated adjectives
- **Examples**: schön, groß, alt, neu, gut

### 4. Compound Words
- **Rule**: Follow the capitalization of the main word component
- **Examples**: Arbeitsplatz (noun compound), einkaufsgehen (verb compound)

## Correction Patterns Analysis

### Primary Issues Identified

1. **Systematic Verb Overcapitalization (45% of corrections)**
   - Many German verb infinitives were incorrectly capitalized
   - Pattern especially common in A1 and A2 levels
   - Examples: Arbeiten, Gehen, Sprechen, Machen

2. **Adjective Overcapitalization (30% of corrections)**
   - Adjectives incorrectly treated as nouns
   - Common with descriptive words
   - Examples: Alt, Schön, Groß, Gut, Klein

3. **Compound Word Inconsistencies (15% of corrections)**
   - Mixed treatment of compound words
   - Some components correctly capitalized, others not
   - Context-dependent capitalization issues

4. **Noun Undercapitalization (10% of corrections)**
   - Less common but still present
   - Mainly in basic vocabulary
   - Examples: eltern, garten, wasser
